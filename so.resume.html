Work Experience

      In my time in the Dublin Engineering and Design Academy, I have had many opportunities to see real world engineering settings. In February, I went on a field trip to Cisco. At Cisco, the tour guides offered us a glimpse of the work culture there, the technology they are creating, and the campus itself. There were many demos of their products. For example, one tool they showed us was a network which could remotely control various settings of a city, such as lighting and humidity.


      The mentorship program also provided me with the opportunity to see several guest speakers. A mentor college forum in December allowed me to listen to various mentors' experiences in education, and how their experiences in high school and college prepared them for the working world. A networking event in May allowed me to talk to more mentors, and listen to everything from their hobbies, their work experience and culture, and to the reason why they chose to enter the field.


      The mentorship program gave me an opportunity to work with a professional in a field of my interest. This year, I worked with Alex Montano, a data architect at Intel. Under his guidance, we met once a month to keep track of long term and short term goals, talk about the college process, and even do mock interviews to prepare for a real world one. The mentorship experience provided me with guidance and advice to pursue engineering further in the future.

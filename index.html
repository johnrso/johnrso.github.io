I am a first year MS CS student at Stanford, where I am fortunate to work with Shuran Song and Jiajun Wu on robot learning. I will also be an AI Resident at 1X this upcoming year, where I will be focusing on humanoid robot learning. I recently finished my BS EECS at UC Berkeley, where I was fortunate to be advised by Pieter Abbeel, Stephen James, and Xingyu Lin as a part of Berkeley's Robot Learning Lab.


Broadly, I am interested in how real-world robots can operate "in the wild"; towards this, my recent research interests have been in efficiently grounding large scale text, image, and video data from pre-trained models into embodiment-specific robotics data. Long term, I am particularly interested in leveraging perspectives from cognitive science and psychology to inform how robots can learn from humans, and how robots can learn like humans.

SpawnNet: Learning Generalizable Visuomotor Skills from Pre-trained Networks

Xingyu Lin*, John So*, Sashwat Mahalingam, Fangchen Liu, Pieter Abbeel

We extract dense features from pre-trained networks to learn generalizable manipulation skills. This shows improvements on categorical generalization against paradigms such as naively using pre-trained representations.

Sim-to-Real via Sim-to-Seg: End-to-end Off-road Autonomous Driving Without Real Data

John So*, Amber Xie*, Sunggoo Jung, Jeffrey Edlund, Rohan Thakker, Ali Agha-mohammadi, Pieter Abbeel, Stephen James

We use photorealistic simulation to learn a segmentation model and train a navigation policy with RL in the learned segmentation space. We deploy zero-shot to a real vehicle.

about


I'm a first year EECS major at UC Berkeley seeking opportunities in computer vision, robotics, and machine learning. I strongly believe that people should put their talents and time towards helping others. I'm also interested in the intersection between technology and social good, including assistive design, accessibility, and STEM education.

My technical background is primarily rooted in competitive robotics, with experience in app development and circuit design. I'm very familiar with working with sensor data/fusion, machine learning models, circuit analysis, and fast prototyping.

Outside of classwork, I'm a course tutor for CS 61A: The Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs, Berkeley's introductory computer science course. I'm also involved with Formula Electric @ Berkeley, Berkeley's Formula SAE EV team, where I'm designing hardware and firmware for circuit boards. I'm hoping to get involved in undergraduate research this semester.
